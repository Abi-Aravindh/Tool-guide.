Tool Guide

Hot Air Rework

Description: A tool that uses hot air to solder or desolder surface-mounted components without direct contact.

How it works: Blows heated air to melt solder on components, allowing removal or placement without damaging the board.

How to use: Adjust temperature/airflow, aim at the component until solder melts, and remove or place components carefully.

Breadboard

Description: A solderless device used to build and test electronic circuits quickly and easily.

How it works: Provides a grid of interconnected sockets to insert and connect electronic components and wires.

How to use: Insert component leads and jumper wires into the sockets according to your circuit design to form connections.

Solder Sucker

Description: A manual vacuum pump used to remove molten solder from a circuit board during desoldering.

How it works: When the plunger is pressed and then released, it creates suction to pull melted solder into the chamber.

How to use: Heat the solder joint until the solder melts, press the plunger down, position the tip over the molten solder, and release the plunger to suck it up.

Surface Plate

Description: A flat, stable reference base used for precision inspection, layout, and tooling setup.

How it works: Offers a smooth, level surface that serves as a reference plane for accurate measurements and marking tasks.

How to use: Place the workpiece securely on the surface plate and use precision measuring instruments to inspect or scribe accurately.

Height Gauge

Description: A precision instrument used to measure vertical distances and mark reference points on a workpiece.

How it works: A vertical scale, often digital, guides a movable scriber or probe to determine height relative to a reference plane.

How to use: Place the height gauge on a surface plate, adjust the scriber or probe to the required height, and read the value from the scale or digital display.

Hardness Tester

Description: A device used to determine a material's hardness by measuring its resistance to indentation or deformation.

How it works: Applies a controlled force through an indenter onto the material surface, then measures the depth or size of the resulting indentation.

How to use: Place the test sample on the platform, position the indenter, apply the required load, and read the hardness value from the scale or digital display.

Coordinate Measuring Machine (CMM)

Description: A precision measurement device used to capture the geometrical dimensions of a part in three-dimensional space.

How it works: A probe moves along X, Y, and Z axes and touches the surface of the object to record precise coordinate data.

How to use: Fix the workpiece securely on the machine's table, calibrate the probe, run the programmed scan or manually guide the probe, and collect measurement data via the system software.

Wire Crimper

Description: A hand tool used to join a wire to a terminal or connector by compressing them together.

How it works: Uses crimping jaws to apply pressure, deforming the connector and securing it tightly to the wire.

How to use: Insert the stripped wire into the terminal, place both into the crimping slot, and firmly squeeze the handles to create a secure connection.

Cable Cutter

Description: A cutting tool designed to cleanly and easily cut through various types of electrical cables.

How it works: Features sharp, curved blades that shear through the cable with applied hand force.

How to use: Position the cable between the jaws of the cutter, then squeeze the handles together to make a clean cut.

Multimeter

Description: A versatile electronic instrument used to measure voltage, current, resistance, and sometimes other parameters like continuity and capacitance.

How it works: Uses internal circuitry to interpret electrical signals received through probes and displays the measurements on a digital or analog interface.

How to use: Select the appropriate measurement mode using the dial, connect the probes to the circuit or component, and read the result on the display.

Non-Contact Voltage Tester

Description: A safety tool used to detect the presence of AC voltage without directly contacting the conductor.

How it works: Employs capacitive sensing to identify the electric field around energized wires or outlets.

How to use: Turn on the tester, bring the tip near the wire, socket, or deviceâ€”if voltage is detected, the tester will emit a light or sound alert.

Clamp Meter

Description: A handheld device used to measure electric current in a conductor without direct contact or disconnecting the circuit.

How it works: Detects current flow using magnetic induction through its clamp jaws surrounding the live conductor.

How to use: Open the clamp, position it around a single current-carrying wire, select the appropriate current range, and read the value on the display.

Circuit Tester

Description: A simple diagnostic tool used to determine whether electrical power is present in outlets, switches, or wiring.

How it works: When connected to a live circuit, the tester lights up or beeps to indicate the presence of voltage.

How to use: Insert the tester into an outlet or touch the probe to a terminal or wire; if voltage is present, it will activate a light or sound signal.

Oscilloscope

Description: An electronic instrument used to display and analyze the waveform of electrical signals.

How it works: Captures and plots voltage signals over time on a screen, allowing visualization of signal behavior.

How to use: Connect the probes to the test points in the circuit, adjust the time/div and volts/div settings, and observe the waveform on the display.

Thermal Camera

Description: A device that visualizes heat by detecting infrared radiation and converting it into an image.

How it works: Senses thermal energy emitted by objects and displays it as a color-coded image representing temperature differences.

How to use: Power on the camera, aim it at the target area, and capture thermal images or video to analyze temperature distribution.
